Burgers equation

The 1D Burgers equation is a simple (if on the simplest) non-linear hyperbolic equation commonly used as a model equation to illustrate various numerical schemes for non-linear hyperbolic differential equations. It is normally prestented as: $$ \begin{equation} \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + u \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = 0 \tag{90} \end{equation} $$

To enable us to present schemes for a greater variety of hyperbolic differenctial equations and to better handle shocks (i.e discontinuities in the solution), we will present our model equation on conservative form: $$ \begin{equation} \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial }{\partial x} \left (\frac{u^2}{2} \right) = 0 \tag{91} \end{equation} $$ and by introducing a flux function $$ \begin{equation} F(u) = \frac{u^2}{2} \tag{92} \end{equation} $$

the conservative formulation of the Burgers equation may be represented by a generic transport equation: $$ \begin{equation} \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial F(u)}{\partial x} = 0 \tag{93} \end{equation} $$

The Lax-Friedrich Scheme

Lax-Friedrichs scheme is an explicit, first order scheme, using forward-difference in time and central-difference in space. However, the scheme is stabilized by averaging \( u^n_i \) over the neighbour cells in the in the temporal approximation: $$ \begin{equation} \frac{u_i^{n+1}-\frac{1}{2}(u^n_{i+1}+u^n_{i-1})}{\Delta t} = -\frac{F^n_{i+1}-F^n_{i-1}}{2 \Delta x} \tag{94} \end{equation} $$ The Lax-Friedrich scheme is the obtained by isolation \( u^{n+1}_i \) at the right hand side: $$ \begin{equation} u_i^{n+1} = \frac{1}{2}(u^n_{i+1}+u^n_{i-1}) - \frac{\Delta t}{2 \Delta x}(F^n_{i+1}-F^n_{i-1}) \label{} \end{equation} $$

Lax-Wendroff Scheme

The Lax-Wendroff scheme may be derived by considering the Taylor series expansion about \( u^{n} \): $$ \begin{equation} u^{n+1} = u^n + u_t \Delta t + \frac{(\Delta t)^2}{2}u_{tt} + \ldots \tag{95} \end{equation} $$ To proceed further, the time partial derivaties must be expressed by means of the PDE to be solved. Now substitute spatial derivatives for the time derivatives

E_t = -AE_x $$u_t = -E_x$$

where \( A = \frac{\partial E}{\partial u} = u \) is the Jacobian for Burgers equation.

This, when plugged into the full Burgers equation, should yield $$\frac{u_i^{n+1} - u_i^n}{\Delta t} = \frac{-E^n_{i+1}-E^n_{i-1}}{2 \Delta x} + \frac{\Delta t}{2} \left(\frac{(A \frac{\partial E}{\partial x})^n_{i+\frac{1}{2}}-(A \frac{\partial E}{\partial x})^n_{i-\frac{1}{2}}}{\Delta x}\right)$$
